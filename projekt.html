About Voskopoja

Voskopoja, or Voskopojë, is a small mountain town located in southeastern Albania near the city of Korçë. It was once one of the most important cultural and commercial centers of the Balkans during the 18th century.

Historical Significance

In the 1700s, Voskopoja was home to the New Academy, one of the first institutions of higher learning in the Balkans, as well as many churches and printing presses. It was a thriving town for Orthodox Christians and played a key role in the spread of education and culture in the region.

Things to See

  • Church of Saint Nicholas – Famous for its frescoes.
  • Church of Saint Athanasius – A beautifully preserved Orthodox church.
  • Old cobblestone streets and traditional stone houses.
  • Hiking trails and mountain views.

Contact & Visit

If you want to visit Voskopoja, the nearest major city is Korçë, only about 20 kilometers away. The area offers small guesthouses and traditional Albanian hospitality.
